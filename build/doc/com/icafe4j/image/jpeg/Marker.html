      • TEM

        public static final Marker TEM
        Define JPEG markers. A marker is prefixed by a one byte segment identifier 0xff. Most markers will have additional information following them. When this is the case, the marker and its associated information is referred to as a "header." In a header the marker is immediately followed by two bytes that indicate the length of the information, in bytes, that the header contains. The two bytes that indicate the length are always included in that count.
